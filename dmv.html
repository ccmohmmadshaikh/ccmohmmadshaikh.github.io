Peak Hours (higher fares)

Weekdays 5:00 – 9:30 AM and 3:00 – 7:00 PM. All other times including evenings, weekends, and federal holidays use off-peak or reduced flat-rate fares. After 9:30 PM and on weekends, fares drop to $2.25–$2.50.

Shared Track Note

The Blue, Orange, and Silver lines share tracks through central DC and across the Potomac. The Green and Yellow lines share tracks through downtown. This means a single station may serve multiple lines — a huge advantage for connections.

    Fares & Payment
    What Does It Cost?

    Metrorail fares are distance-based and time-based. The farther you go and the earlier you travel on weekdays, the more you pay — up to a $6.75 cap.

    Weekday Peak
    $2.25 – $6.75
    Mon–Fri 5–9:30 AM and 3–7 PM. Distance-based. Longer trips cost more.
    Weekday Off-Peak
    $2.25 – $6.75
    Weekday mid-day (9:30 AM–3 PM) and evenings (7–9:30 PM).
    Late Night / Weekend
    $2.25 – $2.50
    After 9:30 PM weeknights and all day Saturday & Sunday. Best value!
    Senior / Disabled
    $1.10 – $3.35
    Half the peak fare at all times. Requires Senior or Disability SmarTrip card.
    Children Under 5
    FREE
    Up to 2 children under age 5 ride free with each paying adult.
    DC Students
    FREE
    DC public, private, charter & parochial school students via Kids Ride Free program.
    How to Pay
    💳
    SmarTrip Card
    Rechargeable card. Buy at any station or at wmata.com. Best option for regular riders. $2 card fee.
    📱
    Mobile SmarTrip
    Add SmarTrip to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet. Tap your phone at the fare gate — no physical card needed.
    💳
    Contactless Card
    Tap any Visa, Mastercard, Amex, or Discover contactless card directly at the gate. Same tap to exit.
    🎟️
    Day / Week Passes
    1-day unlimited ($13), 3-day ($28), 7-day unlimited rail+bus combo available. Great for tourists.
    Important: Always Tap Out

    You must tap your card or phone at the exit gate as well as the entry gate. If you forget to tap out, you will be charged the maximum fare ($6.75) on your next entry. If you're stuck at the exit, press the red button and talk to the station manager.

    Rules & Regulations
    Know Before You Go

    Metro has a firm set of rules. Violations can result in fines up to $300. Here's everything you need to know to ride without issues.

    🚫
    Not Allowed
    No Food or Drink
    Eating and drinking is strictly prohibited on trains and in stations. Even sealed drinks must be put away. Fines apply.
    🐕
    Allowed
    Service Animals Only
    Only trained service animals (ADA-compliant) are permitted. Pet dogs, cats, or other animals must be in a closed carrier bag small enough to fit on your lap.
    🚬
    Not Allowed
    No Smoking or Vaping
    Smoking, e-cigarettes, and vaping devices are prohibited in all Metro facilities — platforms, mezzanines, trains, and parking structures.
    🚲
    Allowed (Rules Apply)
    Bicycles Welcome
    Bikes allowed on trains at most times. Prohibited weekdays 7–10 AM and 4–7 PM. Must use elevator, not escalator. One bike per door.
    🎵
    Use Headphones
    No Loud Music
    Playing music, videos, or audio through speakers is not permitted. Headphones or earbuds are required if you want to listen to media.
    ⚡
    Dangerous
    No E-Scooters / E-Bikes
    Electric scooters, e-bikes, and hoverboards with lithium-ion batteries are not allowed on Metrorail.
    💺
    Courtesy
    Priority Seating
    Seats near the doors are reserved for seniors, disabled riders, and pregnant passengers. Offer your seat without waiting to be asked.
    🧳
    Be Aware
    Large Items & Strollers
    Strollers must be folded before boarding if the train is crowded. Large luggage allowed but be courteous — avoid blocking doorways or aisles.
    🚨
    Illegal
    No Weapons
    Concealed or open carry of firearms is prohibited on Metro property regardless of any permit status. This includes all stations, trains, and parking areas.
    🧍
    The Golden Rule
    Stand Right, Walk Left
    This is the most important unwritten rule in DC. Always stand on the right side of escalators. The left is for people walking or in a hurry. Locals take this seriously.
    📸
    Limited
    Photography Rules
    Personal photography for non-commercial use is generally allowed. Commercial video/photo shoots require a permit from WMATA. No photography in restricted areas.
    👟
    Obstructions
    No Feet on Seats
    Placing feet on seats, sitting across multiple seats, or taking up extra space when the train is crowded is prohibited and subject to fines.

    Accessibility
    Metro for Every Rider

    All 98 Metro stations are fully accessible. Here's what to know before you ride.

    ♿
    Elevators at Every Station
    All stations have elevators from street level to platform. Check elevator status at wmata.com before you go.
    📻
    Audio Announcements
    Every train announces each station stop. Digital displays show the next stop inside every car.
    🟡
    Tactile Warning Strips
    Yellow bumpy strips at the platform edge warn visually-impaired riders of the danger zone near the tracks.
    🚪
    Wide Fare Gates
    Every station has at least one wide gate for wheelchairs and strollers. Press the blue button for the gate to open.
    💛
    Metro Lift (SNAP Discount)
    Customers receiving SNAP benefits qualify for 50% off all Metrorail and Metrobus trips via the Metro Lift program.
    🚐
    MetroAccess Paratransit
    Door-to-door shared-ride service for eligible riders who cannot use fixed-route transit due to disability.
    Metro Glossary
    Learn the Lingo

    New to the Metro? Here are the terms locals and WMATA use that might confuse first-timers.

    SmarTrip
    WMATA's rechargeable payment card (physical or in your phone's wallet). Required for senior/disability discounts and bus-to-rail transfers.
    Faregate
    The turnstile-style gates at station entrances and exits. Tap your card or phone to both enter AND exit.
    Mezzanine
    The middle level of a Metro station — between street level and the platform. Where the faregates and station manager are located.
    Transfer Station
    A station where two or more lines intersect. Examples: Metro Center (Red/Blue/Orange/Silver), Gallery Place (Red/Green/Yellow). Transfers are free.
    Terminus
    The last stop on a line (e.g. Shady Grove on the Red Line). Trains always show the terminus of their direction on the electronic sign.
    Peak Hours
    Mon–Fri 5–9:30 AM and 3–7 PM. Higher fares apply and trains run more frequently during these periods.
    Composite Mile
    WMATA's fare calculation method: the average of the straight-line distance and the over-the-rail distance between two stations.
    Farragut Crossing
    A free transfer between Farragut North (Red) and Farragut West (Blue/Orange/Silver). Exit one, walk 1 block, enter the other — no fare charged.
    Platform
    The lower level where you wait for the train. Metro stations have a distinctive coffered concrete ceiling — one of the most recognizable transit designs in the world.
